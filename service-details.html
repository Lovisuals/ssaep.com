Why This Curriculum Works

Our curriculum is not just theoretical; it aligns with the National Agricultural Science syllabus while introducing modern commercial practices used by successful agripreneurs today.

Integrated Crop & Livestock Management

The core of the SSAEP initiative is to demystify farming. We move beyond "hoe and cutlass" agriculture to teach students the science of yield optimization and animal husbandry. This module runs throughout the academic year, ensuring students witness the full life cycle of their produce.

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Crop Science: Soil testing, bed preparation, organic fertilization, and pest control for vegetables (Ugu, Kale, Cucumber) and staples (Maize, Yam).
  • Livestock Management: Daily routines for Poultry (Broilers/Layers), Catfish (from fingerling to table size), and Piggery.
  • Farm Tools: Proper handling, maintenance, and mechanization of farm implements.
  • Safety First: Mandatory PPE usage and biosecurity protocols for all students.

By the end of the session, students will not only pass their WAEC/NECO practicals with ease but will also have the confidence to manage a small-scale farm business.

Assessment & Certification

Students are assessed based on practical participation (70%) and farm record-keeping (30%). Upon successful completion, participants receive a Certificate of Proficiency in Practical Agriculture from Blessed Farms & Agro Services.
